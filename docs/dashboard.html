CareerScope Overview

CareerScope is a career intelligence dashboard that helps users explore how different master's degrees connect to future careers in the US market. It brings together career stability, salary outlook, demand signals, automation safety, and skill requirements so users can compare degrees, evaluate role options, and identify stronger long term pathways.

Compare degrees clearlySee which degrees look stronger overall in stability, hiring, salary potential, and resilience.
Match degrees to careersUnderstand which jobs align most closely with each degree through GCSI-based ranking.
Spot practical next stepsUse recommendations to identify strong options and areas for upskilling.
What the dashboard measures

It combines labour market indicators with degree to role matching so the user can judge both opportunity quality and relevance at the same time.

What stronger results mean

Higher GCSI, safer automation scores, broader demand, and positive YoY movement usually point to more attractive pathways.

How to interpret recommendations

The highest ranked roles are not just popular. They balance stability and market strength, which makes them more useful for realistic career decision making.

Key terms explained

Overview
GCSI score

The overall career stability score. It combines multiple labour market signals to show how resilient and attractive a field or role looks over time.

Degree alignment

A simple indicator showing how closely a career connects to the selected degree based on the project mapping structure.

YoY

Year over year change. It shows whether a metric such as openings or growth has moved up or down compared with the previous year.

Automation safety

An indicator of how protected a field is from automation pressure. Higher values suggest work that is less exposed to replacement risk.

Demand level

A signal of market activity based on hiring, employment scale, and related opportunity indicators for a field or role.

Recommendation score

The final ranking signal in the recommendations dashboard. Higher values suggest a role is stronger in career stability and market outlook.

Salary range

The expected spread from entry to senior level earnings. It gives a simple view of income progression rather than a single salary snapshot.
